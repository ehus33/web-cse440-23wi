<ProjectSubtitle>Problem</ProjectSubtitle>
 
The overall problem space we explored was that the gym is can be a stressful environment for beginners who don't know how to use equipment and people often find it difficult to manage their time when they're at the gym because of busyness in different sections. Within the gym, there is an internal pressure, particularly against women and people who don't stereotypically fall into the "gym bro" stereotype. This pressure makes it difficult to work out, and often times, this pressure also leads to a hesitance to ask for help when one is struggling. People also find that machines can be super busy at different times of the day and find it difficult to stick to a schedule and remain on time. As a result, this leads to people not pushing themselves as hard, not asking for help, and sometimes even skip their gym workout altogether. This results in people seeing the gym environment as a burden rather than a routine.

<ProjectSubtitle>Design</ProjectSubtitle>
Hence, we designed an application to combat the issue of hostile gym environments and gym hesitancy. We are proposing a gym app that would help to combat this issue. This gym app would be an app that would be specially catered to each gym, and it would include a map of the gym that would help to guide the users, and guide them around the gym most effectively. The app would have two primary functionalities. One of these functionalities is knowing how crowded the gym is before going while measuring the business of specific machines and specific sections of the gym. The other functionality is understanding the various muscle groups and fitness areas that a machine primarily targets and guiding the user through the process of using the machines effectively in a manner that helps them avoid injury.

## Design Research Process and Key Insights

Phase 1: Brainstorming
Given that we had multiple ideas that we wanted to address, we wanted to explore the problem space to see if there were any immediate issues that we could address. Going in, team members identified three different problems each that they thought were problems in society. Then, the problem space was narrowed down to three pressing issues decided by the team through examining problem spaces and comparing the relevance of the issues. The team narrowed the problem selections down to the following three problems:
- Auto Pet Feeder
- Virtual Personal Trainer
- Budget Tracker
While these ideas were vague, and not quite fleshed out, it is important to note that these problems have not had a definitive solution created, but we knew that the solution would have something to do with tracking as that was the selected theme for the course. The only thing we did at that moment in time was identify issues. After a discussion period, the team eventually decided to move forth with the virtual personal trainer.
